Visualizing the worlwide influence of animes:

Exploring the MyAnimeList Social Network

Anime (アニメ, "animation") are a distinctive style of animated storytelling originating from Japan, that often feature vibrant colors, exaggerated facial expressions, and dynamic action sequences. They encompass a wide range of genres and themes, including fantasy, science fiction, romance, comedy, and more. With such a rich and diverse medium, it comes as no surprise that it has gained immense popularity worldwide.

To delve into the expansive world of anime and capture its global influence, our project turns to a treasure trove of data from one of the most popular anime websites in the world. MyAnimeList.net is a social networking platform that allows users to rate and review anime. It also provides a database of information about anime, manga, and animation studios. Evolution in Anime tastes from 2007 to 2018

Since 2007, anime has experienced a consistent rise in popularity. However, over time, preferences in anime have changed, resulting in the development of distinct tastes in various countries.

The first visualization illustrates the evolution of trends in anime over time. Each outer circle corresponds to a specific year, while each inner circle represents a country. The size of each circle is determined by the number of ratings associated with it.

By clicking on any outer circle, you can zoom in to examine it more closely. Additionally, you can click on up to two inner circles, which will display a bar plot on the right panel. Each group in the bar plot represents the top five anime that received the highest number of ratings in a given countryfor a given year.

If an anime name is too long to be fully displayed in the bar plot, it will be partially hidden. However, you can hover over the bar to reveal the complete name and read it.

Variety of Characters in Anime

Many individuals have a tendency to connect with characters in films, shows, books, and anime is no different. That is why, the inclusion of a diverse range of traits for anime characters can be incredibly important.

With this visualization, we seek to demonstrate that anime characters can exhibit both common traits and a diverse range of unique characteristics. Traits are represented by individual words in the word cloud, with their size reflecting the number of characters associated with them. Clicking on any word will highlight it in orange, and the name of the nearest character will be shown in the top left corner of the visualization.

Additionally, there is a search bar located at the top right. If you enter a character's name, it will locate the closest matching name in the dataset and display its traits in red. Words cannot be simultaneously highlighted in both orange and red, so they will appear in blue.

Anime's Global Reach: A passion without borders

Even though it is a Japanese art form, anime defy geographical boundaries, captivating viewers from diverse backgrounds worldwide, transcending gender, generation, religion, race, and nationality.

This choropleth map showcases the distribution of otaku enthusiasts across different countries. Each country is dynamically shaded with colors representing the number of otakus residing within its borders. Darker red means more otakus, while lighter red indicates fewer otakus.

Selecting a country offers some insights into the diverse range of viewers residing in that particular region. This includes information on the most popular animes and studios based on the number of ratings, the gender balance among viewers (including male, female, and non-binary), and the distribution of ages among the audience.

By selecting a studio, you can see some information like the number of animes it produced and the number of countries where its animes are popular. When focusing on a studio, selecting a country reveals the most popular animes from the selected studio within that specific country, demonstrating the popularity and reception of the studio's works among viewers in that particular region.

Variety of Genres in Anime

Genres in anime play a significant role in defining the storytelling, themes, and overall experience of each series. Just like in other forms of entertainment, genres categorize anime into distinct categories based on shared characteristics and themes. Moreover, genres in anime can evolve and blend together, giving rise to unique subgenres and hybrid narratives. This fluidity and this combinaisons allow creators to experiment with different themes, art styles, and storytelling techniques, resulting in a diverse and ever-expanding range of anime experiences.

This last visualization aims to show the relative proportions of genres, identify popular genres, and observe how they are interconnected. By clicking on a particular genre, users can view the genre's distribution within the anime database, enabling them to discover related genres and explore the combinations of genres in anime titles. By clicking on multiple genres, you can narrow down their search and find anime that combine these specific genres, facilitating the discovery of titles that align with their preferences.
