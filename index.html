Evolution in Anime tastes from 2006 to 2018

Since 2006, Anime has experienced a consistent rise in popularity, however over time preferences in Anime have changed, leading to the development of unique tastes in different countries.

The first visualization illustrates the evolution of trends in Anime over time. Each outer circle corresponds to a specific year, while each inner circle represents a country. The size of each circle is determined by the number of ratings associated with it. By clicking on any outer circle, you can zoom in to examine it more closely. Additionally, you can click on up to two inner circles, which will display a bar plot on the right side. Each group in the bar plot represents the top 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 anime for the corresponding inner circles, in comparison to the outer circle. In cases where an anime name is too long to be fully displayed, it will be hidden, but hovering over the bar will reveal the complete name.

Variety of Characters in Anime

Many individuals have a tendency to connect with characters in films, shows, books, and anime is no exception. That is why, the inclusion of a diverse range of traits for anime characters can be very important.

The second visualization aims to show that characters in anime can share many similarities in traits, while also having a wide range of unique characteristics. Traits in the word cloud are depicted by individual words, with their size determined by the number of associated characters. Clicking on any word will highlight it in orange. Upon selecting a word, the name of the nearest character will be displayed in the top left corner. Additionally, there is a search bar located at the top right. If you enter a character's name, it will locate the closest matching name in the dataset and display its traits in red. Words cannot be simultaneously highlighted in both orange and red, so they will appear in blue.

Anime's Global Reach: A passion without borders

Even though it is a Japanese art form, anime defy geographical boundaries, captivating viewers worldwide from diverse backgrounds worldwide, transcending gender, generation, religion, race, and nationality.

This choropleth map showcases the distribution of otaku enthusiasts across different countries. Each country is dynamically shaded with colors representing the number of otakus residing within its borders.

Selecting a country offers some insights into the diverse range of viewers who inhabitate it: the mostpopular animes and studios in terms of the number of ratings, the gender balance, and the distribution of ages.

By selecting a studio, you can see some information like the number of animes it produced and the number of countries where its animes are popular. Selecting a country displays the most popular animes from this studio in this country,highlighting the creative prowess of that studio

Select/click on a country or studio to visualize statistics about it.

Variety of Genres in Anime

Genres in anime play a significant role in defining the storytelling, themes, and overall experience of each series. Just like in other forms of entertainment, genres categorize anime into distinct categories based on shared characteristics and themes. Moreover, genres in anime can evolve and blend together, giving rise to unique subgenres and hybrid narratives. This fluidity and this combinaisons allow creators to experiment with different themes, art styles, and storytelling techniques, resulting in a diverse and ever-expanding range of anime experiences.

The fourth visualization aims to show he relative proportions of genres, identify popular genres, and observe how they are interconnected. By clicking on a particular genre, users can view the genre's distribution within the anime database, enabling them to discover related genres and explore the combinations of genres in anime titles. By clicking on multiple genres, you can narrow down their search and find anime that contain those specific genres, facilitating the discovery of titles that align with their preferences.
